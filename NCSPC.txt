TITLE I: CRIMES AGAINST PERSONS

§101 - Attempted Murder (PC 664/187)

· The intentional attempt to unlawfully kill another human being with malice aforethought.
· Sentence: 5, 7, or 9 years IRL base (300/420/540 mins RP) + $10,000 fine.

§102 - Voluntary Manslaughter (PC 192(a))

· Unlawful killing upon a sudden quarrel or heat of passion.
· Sentence: 3, 6, or 11 years IRL (180/360/660 mins RP) + $10,000 fine.

§103 - Assault with a Deadly Weapon (PC 245(a)(1))

· Assault with a firearm, deadly weapon, or force likely to produce GBI.
· Sentence: 2, 3, or 4 years IRL (120/180/240 mins RP) + $10,000 fine.

§104 - Aggravated Assault (PC 245(a)(4))

· Assault by means of force likely to produce GBI (without a weapon specified).
· Sentence: 1, 2, or 4 years IRL (60/120/240 mins RP) + $10,000 fine.

§105 - Simple Assault / Battery (PC 240/242)

· Unlawful attempt/use of force/violence upon another.
· Sentence: Misdemeanor. Up to 6 months IRL (180 mins RP) jail and/or $2,000 fine.

§106 - Kidnapping (PC 207)

· Unlawful seizure and movement of a person via force/fear.
· Sentence: 3, 5, or 8 years IRL (180/300/480 mins RP) + $10,000 fine. Life for ransom.

§107 - False Imprisonment (PC 236)

· Unlawful violation of personal liberty.
· Sentence: Misdemeanor: Up to 1 year IRL (360 mins RP) jail. Felony (violence/fraud): 16 mo/2/3 yrs IRL.

§108 - Robbery (PC 211)

· Taking property from person/presence by force/fear.
· Sentence: 2, 3, or 5 years IRL (120/180/300 mins RP) + $10,000 fine. +1 year if armed.

§109 - Criminal Threats (PC 422)

· Threat to cause death/GBI causing sustained fear.
· Sentence: Misdemeanor or Felony (16 mo/2/3 yrs IRL).

§110 - Stalking (PC 646.9)

· Course of conduct causing credible threat and fear.
· Sentence: Misdemeanor or Felony (16 mo/2/3 yrs IRL).

§303 - Cultivation of Cannabis (HSC 11358)

· Cultivation of more than 6 living plants per adult, or any cultivation for sale without license.
· Sentence: Misdemeanor (6+ plants personal) or Felony (sale).

§304 - Possession of Controlled Substance (HSC 11350) - NON-CANNABIS

· Possession of cocaine, heroin, methamphetamine, MDMA, etc.
· Sentence: Misdemeanor. Up to 1 year IRL jail (360 mins RP).

§305 - Possession for Sale (HSC 11351) - NON-CANNABIS

· Possession of controlled substances with intent to sell.
· Sentence: Felony. 2, 3, or 4 years IRL (120/180/240 mins RP).

§306 - Transportation/Sale (HSC 11352) - NON-CANNABIS

§307 - Manufacturing (HSC 11379.6) - NON-CANNABIS

TITLE VII: SENTENCING FRAMEWORK & PAROLE

Standard IRL to In-Game Time Conversion: 1 IRL minute = 1 in-game day served.

Parole (PC 3000):

· Mandatory after release from a state prison sentence.
· Parole period = 3 IRL days for most felonies, 5 IRL days for violent felonies.
· Parolee must check in with Parole Agent and obey all conditions (no firearms, submit to search, maintain employment RP).

Probation:

· A court-ordered alternative to jail. Period = 1 to 5 IRL days.
· Probationer must check in with Probation Officer and avoid new arrests.

Fines: Use real California base fines + penalties. Example structure for server economy:

· Infraction: $100 - $500
· Misdemeanor: $1,000 - $3,000
· Felony: $5,000 - $20,000+

Three Strikes Law (PC 667): (Optional for high-realism)

· A defendant with two or more prior "strike" convictions (violent/serious felonies) faces a mandatory sentence of 25 years to life IRL (a very long, essentially "perma-character" RP sentence for extreme repeat offenders).

1. TRAFFIC & VEHICLE CODE

DUI Procedures

· Field Sobriety Testing (FST): Mandatory for suspected impairment. Includes Horizontal Gaze Nystagmus (HGN), Walk-and-Turn, One-Leg Stand. Refusal = automatic arrest.
· Chemical Testing: Breathalyzer (PAS) at scene. Blood test at hospital if suspect refuses or is unconscious. Refusal = 1-year license suspension + enhanced charges.
· Impound: Vehicle impounded for 30 days (RP: 30 IRL minutes) for DUI arrest.
· Tiered Penalties:
  · 1st Offense: Misdemeanor. 48 hrs - 6 months IRL jail (96-180 mins RP), $1,500 fine, 6-month license suspension.
  · 2nd Offense (within 10 years): Misdemeanor. 96 hrs - 1 year IRL jail (192-360 mins RP), $2,800 fine, 2-year license suspension.
  · 3rd Offense: Felony. 120 days - 1 year IRL jail (240-360 mins RP), $5,000 fine, 3-year license revocation.
  · DUI with Injury: Felony. 16 months - 4 years IRL prison (96-240 mins RP), $10,000 fine, 5-year revocation.

Reckless Driving

· Definition: Willful/wanton disregard for safety of persons/property.
· Standard: Misdemeanor. 5-90 days IRL jail (10-180 mins RP), $1,000 fine.
· With Injury: "Reckless Driving Causing Injury" - Felony. 16 months - 3 years IRL (96-180 mins RP), $5,000 fine.
· Exhibition of Speed: Infraction. $250 fine, vehicle impound 7 days.

Hit-and-Run

· Property Damage Only (VC 20002): Misdemeanor. Up to 6 months IRL jail (180 mins RP), $1,000 fine.
· With Injury (VC 20001): Felony. 2-4 years IRL prison (120-240 mins RP), $10,000 fine.
· With Death: Felony. 4-6 years IRL prison (240-360 mins RP), $20,000 fine.
· Mandatory: Report accident to LEO within 24 hours RP.

Vehicle Registration & Licensing

· Expired Registration: Infraction. $100 fine + $50 per month overdue.
· No Registration: Misdemeanor. $500 fine, vehicle towed.
· Driving Without License: Infraction. $250 fine.
· Driving on Suspended/Revoked: Misdemeanor. 10 days - 1 year IRL jail (20-360 mins RP), $1,500 fine.
· Commercial Licensing: Required for trucks, buses. Violation = $1,000 fine.

2. COURT & LEGAL PROCEDURES

Arrest → Booking → Trial Workflow

```
1. ARREST: 
   - Mirandize suspect (if interrogating)
   - Transport to nearest station/holding

2. BOOKING:
   - Photograph, fingerprints, property inventory
   - Criminal record check
   - Medical screening (EMS if injured)
   - Cell assignment based on severity

3. INITIAL APPEARANCE:
   - Within 48 IRL minutes of arrest
   - Judge advises charges, rights
   - Bail determination

4. PRELIMINARY HEARING:
   - 3 IRL days after arrest
   - DA presents evidence
   - Judge determines if case proceeds

5. ARRAIGNMENT:
   - Defendant enters plea
   - Trial date set (7 IRL days for misdemeanor, 14 for felony)

6. TRIAL:
   - Jury selection (6-12 players for felonies)
   - Presentation of evidence
   - Witness testimony
   - Closing arguments
   - Jury deliberation (max 30 IRL minutes)
   - Verdict

7. SENTENCING:
   - 3 IRL days after conviction
   - Judge imposes sentence per NCSPC
```

Bail/Bond System

· Bail Schedule:
  · Misdemeanor: $2,500 - $25,000
  · Felony: $25,000 - $1,000,000
  · Violent Felony: No bail or $500,000+
· 10% Bond Option: Pay 10% to bondsman for release.
· OR Release: Own Recognizance for minor first offenses.

Evidence Handling

· Chain of Custody: Required log for all evidence.
· Storage: Evidence locker with officer's name, date, case number.
· DNA/Fingerprints: Sent to state lab (RP: results in 7 IRL days).
· Body Cam: Mandatory for all LEO interactions. Footage stored 90 days.

Court Roles

· Judge: Presides over court, rules on objections, sentences.
· District Attorney (DA): Prosecutes cases, files charges.
· Public Defender (PD): Defends indigent clients.
· Clerk: Manages docket, swears in witnesses, maintains records.
· Bailiff: Court security, transports prisoners.
· Jury: 6 members for misdemeanor, 12 for felony. Selected from player pool.

3. LAW ENFORCEMENT SOPs

Arrest Procedures

1. Probable Cause: Required for arrest.
2. Miranda Warning: Required before interrogation.
3. Handcuffing: Standard for felony arrests, discretionary for misdemeanor.
4. Search Incident to Arrest: Permissible of person and immediate area.
5. Inventory Search: Required of vehicle upon impound.

Use-of-Force Matrix

```
LEVEL 1: Verbal Commands
LEVEL 2: Soft Empty Hand Control (wrist locks, pressure points)
LEVEL 3: Hard Empty Hand Control (takedowns, strikes)
LEVEL 4: Intermediate Weapons (baton, taser, OC spray)
LEVEL 5: Lethal Force (firearms)

ESCALATION: Must attempt de-escalation before moving up levels.
JUSTIFICATION: Force must be objectively reasonable for the threat.
```

Pursuit Rules

· Authorization Required: Supervisor must approve pursuits.
· Termination Conditions:
  · Speeds exceed 120 mph
  · Residential/school zones
  · Extreme danger to public
  · Air unit available
· PIT Maneuver: Only for violent felonies with supervisor approval.

Citation vs. Arrest Decision Matrix

```
             │ Minor Offense │ Serious Offense │ Violent Offense
─────────────┼───────────────┼─────────────────┼───────────────
Cooperative  │ Citation      │ Arrest          │ Arrest
─────────────┼───────────────┼─────────────────┼───────────────
Uncooperative│ Arrest        │ Arrest          │ Arrest + Force
─────────────┼───────────────┼─────────────────┼───────────────
Fleeing      │ Pursue/Cite   │ Pursue/Arrest   │ Pursue/Arrest
```

9. SCENARIO & ROLEPLAY CONTEXT

Common RP Situation Guidelines

TRAFFIC STOP:

```
1. Officer: Lights on, position safely
2. Driver: Pull over, hands on wheel
3. Officer: Approach, request license/registration
4. Driver: Provide documents, state purpose of travel
5. Officer: Run checks, decide citation/arrest
6. Driver: Comply or face escalation
```

PURSUIT:

```
1. Initiation: Lights/siren, suspect fails to yield
2. Pursuit: Maintain visual, call for backup
3. Termination: PIT/spike strips/roadblock if authorized
4. Arrest: High-risk stop procedure, K9 if needed
```

ROBBERY IN PROGRESS:

```
1. Dispatch: Code 211, location, suspect description
2. Response: Multiple units, perimeter established
3. Containment: Secure exits, hostage negotiator if needed
4. Resolution: Arrest or lethal force if immediate threat
```

DOMESTIC VIOLENCE:

```
1. Response: Two officers minimum
2. Assessment: Separate parties, check for injuries
3. Determination: Primary aggressor identified
4. Arrest: If probable cause, mandatory arrest for violence
5. Resources: Provide shelter information, restraining order
```

10. EXCEPTIONS & SPECIAL RULES

Minors (Under 21)

· Arrest: Transport to juvenile hall, notify parents
· Sentencing: Juvenile court, focus on rehabilitation
· Records: Sealed at 18 unless violent felony
· Curfew: 10 PM - 6 AM for under 18 ($100 fine to parents)

Public Officials

· Mayor/Judge/Sheriff: Immune from minor traffic citations while on duty
· Arrest: Requires supervisor approval + internal affairs notification
· Special Handling: Transport to separate holding, expedited bail

Gang-Related Activity

· Enhancements: +5 years for crimes committed for gang
· Database: Gang member registry
· Trespassing: Automatic gang injunctions in certain areas
· Probation: No contact with known gang members

Edge Cases & Unusual Situations

MENTAL HEALTH CRISIS:

```
1. CIT Trained Officer required
2. De-escalation priority
3. Transport to psychiatric facility, not jail
4. 72-hour hold for evaluation
```

SOVEREIGN CITIZEN:

```
1. Do not argue ideology
2. Issue citations based on observable violations
3. Arrest if non-compliant
4. Let courts handle legal arguments
```

OFF-DUTY OFFICER INVOLVEMENT:

```
1. Identify as officer immediately
2. Take command if no on-duty present
3. Follow same use-of-force rules
4. File report as involved citizen
```

ANIMAL-RELATED INCIDENTS:

```
1. Animal Control primary for non-dangerous
2. LEO for dangerous animals
3. Shoot only if immediate human threat
4. Owner liable for damages/injuries
```
